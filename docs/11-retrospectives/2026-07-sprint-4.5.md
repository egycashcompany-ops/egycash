## 1. Sprint goal

Deliver **Stage 5 (Employee Creation)** of the approved seven-stage recruitment workflow: turn
an applicant whose Job Offer was Accepted into an Employee, hired from the offer's immutable
Accepted Snapshot — strictly Stage 5, additive, touching no later stage.

## 2. Delivered features

- **Employee aggregate** (`hr_employees`) created from an **Accepted** Job Offer only.
- **Employment terms read exclusively from the immutable Accepted Snapshot** (never the live,
  mutable offer) — the snapshot frozen in Stage 4 is the sole source of the hired terms.
- **Unique employee number** `EMP-{YYYY}-{seq:6}` — organization-wide, atomic per-year counter
  in the shared `hr_sequences` collection, unique-index backstop; searchable in the list
  endpoint.
- **Atomic creation** — the number allocation and the record insert run in a single
  transaction (`unitOfWork`); a **unique index on `jobOfferId`** prevents a duplicate employee
  from the same offer even under concurrency, with a fast-path service check.
- **Preserved references** to the Applicant, the Job Requisition (carried by the applicant),
  and the Accepted Job Offer; **copied employment terms** plus the accepted offer revision
  number; initial status **`active`**; recorded **hiring date** (defaults to now).
- **Publishes** `hr.employee.created`, **notifies** the reporting manager + the creator, and
  **audits** every operation. Permissions `employee.{view,create}`; route `/hr/employees`.

## 5. Architecture review summary

Self-review against the module-structure checklist plus EGYCASH's independent review —
**approved, no blocking comments**. The transaction boundary, the immutable Accepted Snapshot
sourcing, employee numbering, preserved references, duplicate protection, events,
notifications, and audit were all confirmed correct. The module boundary held (no

their `index.ts` barrels; platform Notifications and `unitOfWork` via `platform/*`); the
collection carries the `hr_` prefix.

## 7. Lessons learned

1. **The Accepted Snapshot paid its dividend downstream.** Because Stage 4 froze the accepted
   terms into an immutable snapshot, Stage 5 had a single, unambiguous source to copy from —
   no "live vs accepted terms" reconciliation, and the hire is reproducible from one field.
2. **A real transaction was the right tool for "create atomically".** Allocating the number and
   inserting the record inside `unitOfWork` means a duplicate-offer conflict rolls the number
   back instead of leaving a gap, and the unique `jobOfferId` index makes the one-employee-per-
   offer guarantee hold under concurrency rather than relying on a check-then-write race.
3. **The recruitment pipeline composed cleanly end to end.** Five stages now chain through the
   aggregates' public barrels and their gate methods (screening-accepted → interviews-cleared →
   offer-accepted → hired) with no rewrites — evidence the one-capability-per-sprint discipline
   and the module-feature boundaries scaled across the whole workflow.
